Getting Started with Grok

Your go-to AI for finding parts, prices, and real-time information. Like having a mechanic and parts counter guy who knows the whole internet.

Stop calling three dealers for a part number. Ask Grok — it checks them all at once. AI searches the entire internet in seconds. Faster and smarter than any phone call.

What Is Grok?

Grok is an AI assistant made by xAI (Elon Musk's AI company). It lives inside X (formerly Twitter) but also has its own website. What makes Grok different is that it can search the internet in real time. That means it can find:

  • Parts and equipment — specific model numbers, compatibility, who has them in stock
  • Current prices — what things actually cost right now, not last year
  • Local dealers and sellers — who's near you, what's on their lot
  • Technical specs — torque specs, fluid capacities, part numbers, manual info
  • Recent news and posts — what people are saying right now on X/Twitter

It's free to use and great for the kind of quick searches farmers need every day.

How to Get Started

1

Go to Grok

Open your web browser and go to:

grok.com

You can also access Grok through the X (Twitter) app if you have an account.

2

Sign In or Create Account

You can sign in with your X (Twitter) account, Google account, or Apple account. If you don't have any of these, you can create a free X account in a couple minutes.

3

Start Asking Questions

Type your question in the message box. Grok will search the internet and come back with answers, links, and sources. It's like Google but it actually gives you the answer instead of a list of websites to dig through.

Example: Find Parts for Your Equipment

Let's walk through finding a hydraulic cylinder for an older tractor. This is the kind of thing that can take hours on Google — Grok can do it in seconds.

1

Describe What You Need

Be as specific as you can — include the make, model, year, and what the part is for.

You Type: I need a hydraulic cylinder for the front-end loader on a 1978 John Deere 4440. The cylinder is leaking and I need to either find a replacement or a rebuild kit. I'm near Haxtun, Colorado. What are my options?
Grok Responds With: A detailed answer including: the specific cylinder part numbers for that loader model, links to aftermarket suppliers like Hy-Capacity and Surplus Center, rebuild kit options with prices, nearby hydraulic repair shops in Sterling and Fort Morgan, and salvage yards in eastern Colorado that might have them.
2

Compare Prices

Ask Grok to help you shop around.

You Type: What's cheaper — buying a new aftermarket cylinder or getting a rebuild kit? Give me rough prices for both options.
Grok Responds With: A price comparison showing new aftermarket cylinders ($250-400 depending on supplier), rebuild kits ($50-120), and the pros and cons of each option — like how a rebuild is cheaper but takes time, and a new one gets you running faster.
3

Find It Locally

Get Grok to search for what's available near you right now.

You Type: Are there any salvage yards or used equipment dealers within 100 miles of Haxtun, CO that might have JD 4440 parts? Also check if anyone on X/Twitter is selling JD 4440 parts.
Grok Responds With: A list of salvage yards (like those in Sterling, Yuma, or Sidney NE), used equipment dealers, and any relevant posts from X/Twitter where people are selling 4440 parts. Includes addresses and phone numbers.
4

Get Technical Help

Use Grok to look up specs and procedures.

You Type: What's the procedure to remove and reinstall the loader cylinder on a JD 4440? Any special tools needed? What hydraulic fluid does it take?
Grok Responds With: Step-by-step removal procedure, the tools you'll need (probably a 1-1/16" wrench and hydraulic line wrenches), the correct hydraulic fluid spec (Hy-Gard or equivalent), and capacity information.

If you want the answer, ask AI. It's smarter than anyone you can call. Parts, prices, specs, troubleshooting — Grok finds it in seconds, not hours.
