FBA Application Guide

Step-by-step instructions for completing and submitting your Farm Bill Application through your local FSA office.

Enrollment Deadline: April 17, 2026

What Is the Farm Bill Application (FBA)?

The Farm Bill Application (CCC-866) is the form you submit to enroll your farm base acres in either ARC or PLC for the current crop year. This election determines how your safety-net payments are calculated if commodity prices or revenues fall below certain levels.

To complete enrollment, you will need:

  • A verified Login.gov account linked to Farmers.gov
  • Your Farm Number(s) and Tract Number(s)
  • Names and signatures of all producers on the farm
  • Your ARC or PLC election for each covered commodity
1

Log In to Farmers.gov

Go to farmers.gov and sign in with your Login.gov credentials. If you haven't set up your Login.gov account yet, complete that first using our Login.gov Setup Guide.

  1. Go to farmers.gov
  2. Click "Sign In" in the top right
  3. Choose "Login.gov" as your sign-in method
  4. Enter your Login.gov email and password
  5. Complete two-factor authentication

First time? If this is your first time signing in to Farmers.gov, you may need to verify your identity. See our Login.gov guide for help with that process.

2

Navigate to the Farm Bill Application

Once signed in, find the Farm Bill Application section.

  1. From the Farmers.gov dashboard, look for "Farm Programs" or "Farm Bill"
  2. Select "Agriculture Risk Coverage / Price Loss Coverage (ARC/PLC)"
  3. Click "Start a New Application" or "Continue Application"
3

Select Your Farms

Select the farm numbers you want to enroll. Each farm will show its base acres and covered commodities. You will make your ARC or PLC election for each commodity on each farm.

  1. Review the list of farms associated with your account
  2. Check the box next to each farm you want to enroll
  3. Verify the base acres and commodities listed are correct

Check your base acres. If your base acres or commodities look incorrect, contact your local FSA office before proceeding. Errors in base data should be resolved before you enroll.

Key Decision

ARC vs. PLC: Which to Choose?

For each covered commodity on each farm, you must elect either ARC-CO (Agriculture Risk Coverage - County) or PLC (Price Loss Coverage). This election is locked in for the crop year.

ARC-CO PLC
Trigger County revenue falls below benchmark National price falls below reference price
Based on 5-year Olympic average of county revenue Statutory reference price set by Congress
Best when Prices are strong but yields are at risk Prices are expected to drop below reference
Payment cap 10% of benchmark revenue No cap on per-bushel payment rate

Talk to Kyle if you need help deciding. The right choice depends on your specific crops, county yield history, and current market conditions. Call (970) 571-1015 to discuss your options.

4

Make Your ARC/PLC Election

For each commodity on each farm, select either ARC-CO or PLC. Review your selections carefully before proceeding.

  1. For each farm, review the covered commodities listed
  2. Select ARC-CO or PLC for each commodity
  3. Confirm your selections on the summary screen

This election is annual. Unlike previous farm bills, the 2018 Farm Bill allows you to change your ARC/PLC election each year. However, once you enroll for a crop year, that election is final.

5

Review and Sign

Review all the information on your application. All producers listed on the farm must sign the application for enrollment to be complete.

  1. Review the application summary — farms, commodities, and elections
  2. Electronically sign the application
  3. If there are other producers on the farm, they must also sign
  4. Submit the completed application

All signatures required. The application is not complete until every producer on the farm has signed. If you have partners or landlords on the farm, coordinate with them to ensure all signatures are collected before the deadline.

6

Confirm Submission

After all signatures are collected and the application is submitted, you should receive a confirmation.

  1. Look for a confirmation number or receipt on screen
  2. Save or print the confirmation for your records
  3. Check your email for a submission confirmation
  4. Follow up with your FSA office if you don't receive confirmation within a few business days

Need Help?

Contact Kyle McConnell or your local FSA office if you have questions about your enrollment or need help with the application process.
