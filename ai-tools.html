Get Started with AI

Three powerful tools that can save you time and money on your operation. No tech experience needed — just type what you need.

What Is AI and Why Should You Care?

AI assistants are like having a knowledgeable helper available 24/7. You type a question or request in plain English, and they give you a useful answer. Think of it like texting a really smart friend who never sleeps. Each tool has different strengths — here's which one to use for what.

✨

Claude

Best for Planning & Writing

Made by Anthropic. Excellent at thinking through business plans, writing professional documents, and analyzing your operation's numbers.

Example Use "Help me create a marketing plan for my custom harvesting business in NE Colorado."

Tips for Talking to AI

Be Specific

Instead of "help with marketing," say "write a Facebook post about our Pioneer seed plot day on June 15 in Haxtun." The more details, the better the answer.

Give Context

Tell the AI who you are: "I'm a farmer in NE Colorado with 2,000 dryland acres." This helps it give you relevant, local answers.

Ask Follow-Ups

Don't settle for the first answer. Say "make it shorter," "add pricing," or "now do one for soybeans." The conversation keeps going.

Double-Check Facts

AI is a great starting point, but always verify important numbers, dates, and claims — especially for chemical rates, regulations, or legal matters.
