Use AI to build the most powerful, private farm management system you've ever had. Your data, your rules, your competitive advantage.

Imagine This

Imagine you have a private website — one that only you can access — where everything about your operation lives in one place. Not a spreadsheet. Not a pile of papers in a filing cabinet. Not an app some company controls. Your own system, built exactly the way you think.

What Your Farm Database Could Track

  • Every field — location, acres, soil type, irrigation, landlord, lease terms
  • Every input — seed, fertilizer, chemical, rates, costs, dates applied
  • Every yield — bushels per acre, moisture, test weight, by field, by year
  • Every piece of equipment — make, model, hours, maintenance history, parts replaced

All searchable. All sortable. All connected. Ask questions like "What was my average cost per bushel of corn across all irrigated fields in 2025?" and get an instant answer. No farm management software on the market gives you this much control for this little money.

Why Build Your Own Instead of Using Granular, Bushel, or FarmLogs?

  • You own your data — With commercial farm software, your data lives on their servers. If they get bought, go bankrupt, change pricing, or shut down, your data could disappear. With your own database, your data is yours forever.
  • No subscription trap — Farm software runs $500-3,000+/year. Your own system costs ~$7-27/month, and you're not locked in.
  • Built exactly for you — Commercial tools are designed for the average farmer. Your system tracks what you care about, organized the way you think.
  • Private and secure — Your yield data, your financials, your landlord terms — none of it goes through a company that might sell aggregated data or get hacked.
  • AI-powered analysis — Connect Claude to your data and ask questions in plain English. No farm software does this yet.
Think of it this way Using commercial farm software is like renting ground — you farm it, but someone else owns it and sets the terms. Building your own database is like owning the land. More work upfront, but it's yours.

Keeping Your Data Sacred: Security

This is the most important section. Your farm's financial data, yield data, and landlord agreements are sensitive. Here's how to keep them private.

Authentication: Google or Microsoft Login

Instead of creating a username and password (which can be guessed, stolen, or forgotten), your farm database uses OAuth authentication — signing in with your Google or Microsoft account. Here's why this is better:

Google Sign-In

Use your existing Gmail or Google account

  • Two-factor authentication built in
  • Google handles password security
  • One click to sign in
  • Free to implement
  • Works on phone and desktop

Microsoft Sign-In

Use your Outlook, Hotmail, or work account

  • Enterprise-grade security
  • Microsoft handles password security
  • Familiar if you use Office 365
  • Free to implement
  • Works on phone and desktop

How This Keeps You Safe

Only your account can access your data. When you set up Google or Microsoft authentication, you configure it so that only your specific email address (e.g., john@gmail.com) is allowed in. Nobody else — even if they know the website address — can see anything.

Your password is never stored in your app. Google/Microsoft handle the login. Your app never sees or stores your password. This means even if someone hacked your Render server, they couldn't find your password — it doesn't exist there.

Two-factor authentication (2FA) comes free. If you have 2FA enabled on your Google or Microsoft account (and you should), that protection automatically applies to your farm database too.

HTTPS encryption. Render automatically provides HTTPS (the lock icon in your browser). This means all data between your computer and your database is encrypted — even on public Wi-Fi at the co-op.

MongoDB Atlas security. Your database on MongoDB Atlas is encrypted at rest (stored encrypted on disk), encrypted in transit (encrypted when moving between servers), and requires authentication to access. It meets the same security standards used by banks and hospitals.

Important: While this setup is very secure for a personal/family farm database, it's not the same as a bank or medical system. Don't store Social Security numbers, bank account passwords, or other extremely sensitive credentials in any system you build yourself. Use this for farm operations data — fields, yields, costs, plans, equipment — which is exactly what it's designed for.

Push your code to GitHub, connect Render to your repo, add your MongoDB connection string and Google OAuth keys. Claude will give you the exact steps.

5

Keep Adding Modules

Once the foundation is working, add modules one at a time. Each conversation with Claude builds on what you already have. Over a few weekends, you'll have a complete farm management system.
